About Me Security Enthusiast

I'm currently working as a VAPT Consultant - basically I get paid to break into things before the bad guys do. Day to day that means being part of red team exercises, running internal and external penetration tests, and digging into mobile and web apps to find what's hiding under the surface.

On the web app side I've turned up some solid findings - multiple critical vulnerabilities across different clients, everything from IDORs to pretty nasty business logic errors that could've caused real damage if left unpatched. It's the kind of stuff that doesn't always show up in automated scans, which is why the manual testing matters.

Outside of client work I keep things sharp with CTFs on TryHackMe and studying Blue Teaming - understanding how defenders think makes you a better attacker. OSCP was the milestone that kicked this all off properly, and I'm not done collecting certs just yet.

Skills Technical Arsenal

Threat Intelligence & Analysis

I track attacks, read traffic, and connect the dots so the bigger picture makes sense fast.

Network Security

Networks are almost always the first thing I'm looking at on an engagement. Understanding firewalls, IDS/IPS, and how protocols behave under the hood makes finding misconfigurations a lot less guesswork.

Penetration Testing

This is the core of what I do. From initial recon all the way through exploitation and reporting - web apps, internal networks, external perimeters, mobile. If there's a way in, the goal is to find it first.

System Administration

Running a homelab forces you to actually understand how systems are put together. Spinning up servers, managing containers, and configuring services gives me a different perspective when I'm on the other side trying to break them.

Cloud Computing

Cloud environments are everywhere in client infrastructure now. Having a solid grasp of AWS, GCP, and Azure - especially IAM - means I know where the misconfigurations tend to hide.

Development & Automation

Writing scripts and small tools saves a lot of time on repetitive tasks. Python and Bash are my go-tos for automating recon, parsing output, and building quick utilities when something off-the-shelf doesn't quite fit.
